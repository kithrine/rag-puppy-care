House training works best when you put your puppy on a consistent schedule and stick to it. Puppies thrive on routine, and predictable meals, potty breaks, naps, and playtime all make it easier for them to learn where and when to go. It won't happen overnight, so patience and consistency matter more than anything else.

A young puppy can only hold its bladder for so long. A good rule of thumb is the month plus one rule: take your puppy's age in months and add one, and that's roughly the maximum number of hours they can comfortably hold it. So an 8 week old (2 month) puppy maxes out around three hours, a 12 week old around four hours, and so on. Cap it around six hours once they're an adult. Younger puppies need to go out far more often than this maximum during active daytime hours.

There are predictable times your puppy will almost always need to potty. Take them out first thing in the morning, after every meal, after every nap, after play sessions, before crate time, and last thing before bed. After a meal, the window is short, usually somewhere between 5 and 30 minutes, and the younger the puppy the sooner they'll need to go. Watch them after they drink water too and treat it the same as a meal.

Start every day the same way. When you wake up, get your puppy out of the crate and straight outside before you do anything else. Don't stop for coffee or to check your phone. With a small puppy you can carry them out so they don't have an accident on the way to the door. Always head out the same door to the same spot so the location becomes part of the routine.

Keep your puppy on a leash outside during training, even in a fenced yard. This keeps them focused on the task instead of wandering off to play, and it lets you see exactly what happened so you can react. Wait quietly for them to go rather than turning it into a walk right away. The moment they finish, reward them with calm praise and a treat so they connect going outside with good things.

Learn to read your puppy's signals. Sudden quiet, circling, sniffing the floor, or heading toward a door are all signs they need to go. Most puppies start showing these pre potty signals within a couple weeks of consistent training. If you catch the signal and get them outside in time, every success builds the habit faster.

Accidents are going to happen, and they're almost always a missed schedule rather than a training failure. Never punish your puppy for an accident and never rub their nose in it. Punishment based methods actually slow house training down and can teach the puppy to hide when they go instead of going in the right spot. If you catch them mid accident, calmly interrupt and get them outside. If you find it after the fact, just clean it up.

Clean accidents with an enzyme based cleaner, not a regular household cleaner. Standard cleaners don't fully break down the proteins in urine, so the puppy can still smell the old spot and will be drawn back to go there again. Enzyme cleaners like Nature's Miracle break those down so the spot doesn't keep pulling them back.

Most puppies are reliably house trained within a couple months of consistent work, though it varies by breed and by how steady you are with the schedule. Small breeds often take a bit longer because they have smaller bladders. A 6 month old puppy is usually dependable most of the time. If your puppy seems stuck or is having way more accidents than expected, it's worth a vet visit to rule out a urinary tract infection or other health issue.
